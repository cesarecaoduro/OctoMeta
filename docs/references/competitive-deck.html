§ 01 · The problem

Every engineering deliverable is built three times.

  • The calc lives in Excel: logic hidden behind cells, units unchecked, unreviewable.
  • The report lives in Word: numbers pasted in, stale the moment loading changes.
  • The model lives in CAD: reconciled with the calc by hand, if at all.

§ 02 · Why now

Three waves just made this buildable, and buyable.

  • Browser CAD came of age. WASM B-Rep kernels mean real parametric geometry in a web tab: no desktop install, no per-seat CAD license.
  • Digital delivery mandates. IFC/openBIM requirements are landing in public procurement. Firms must ship structured data, not PDFs of screenshots.
  • AI needs a substrate it can't hallucinate through. Copilot writing into opaque cells makes the reviewability crisis worse. A typed, unit-safe dependency graph is the surface AI can safely act on, and be audited on.

The window: nobody has combined all three. The first product that does becomes the system of record for engineering intent.

§ 08 · The real differentiator

Everyone owns a piece. Nobody owns the graph.

Our differentiator is not a feature; it's the substrate: one typed, unit-safe dependency graph that spans number → geometry → document. Everything else falls out of it:

  • 01 Geometry is a first-class value: a beam is a node, computed from formulas, rendered as live B-Rep.
  • 02 The deliverable is a projection, not a file: PDF and IFC4X3 export from the same graph, so they cannot disagree.
  • 03 Spreadsheet muscle memory on top; typed, reviewable, order-independent semantics underneath.
  • 04 AI-ready by architecture: one mutation API + provenance on every node. Honest: AI ships later, on rails.

Why it compounds: every template, every verified calc, every firm standard encoded in the graph raises switching costs. Copying one feature doesn't copy the substrate.
